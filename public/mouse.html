// FAQ

QUESTIONS YOU MIGHT HAVE

When can I actually use Pixel Mouse?
Realistically: late 2026 for v1.0. Early alpha builds: Sep-Oct 2026 for first 50 waitlist members. Public beta: November 2026. I'm one person with a day job, building this in evenings and weekends. No fake "next week" promises. Real timelines.
Will it be free?
Free tier: yes. Auto-click, simple macros, single-monitor. Pro tier: $4-7/mo for advanced features (image matching, scripting, multi-monitor, {BOT} customization). Early supporters get founding price ($2/mo lifetime). During beta: free for everyone.
Mac / Linux support?
Windows first (v1.0). Mac v1.1 (early 2027). Linux later if there's demand. One indie dev = focused scope. I can't ship 3 platforms simultaneously and not break.
Why not use AutoHotkey / existing tools?
AutoHotkey is amazing for power users. But not everyone is a power user. Pixel Mouse is for people who want automation without the script learning curve. Drag-and-drop workflows. Visual macros. Friendly UI. Zero coding required.
Is this a scam? Why no .exe yet?
Fair question. No, not a scam. Yes, design comes before code. Pixel Remover (the proof I ship) is live now: toolkit.pebblylabs.com. If I disappear, you get nothing. If I succeed, you get the product. That's the deal.
What about anti-virus warnings?
Honest answer: Yes, indie automation apps without code-signing certificates often trigger Windows Defender. For v1.0, expect "Unknown Publisher" warnings. Source, hashes, and VirusTotal scans will be published openly. Code-signing certificate later when there's revenue to support it.
Will you sell my data?
No. Pebbly philosophy: your data stays on your device. Pixel Mouse processes locally. No telemetry beyond opt-in crash reports. Email signup is for product updates only. Unsubscribe any time.
